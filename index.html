Golden Temple

Punjab
The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a revered Sikh temple located in Amritsar, Punjab. It is one of the most spiritually significant places for Sikhs worldwide. The temple's stunning architecture, adorned with gold leafing, attracts millions of visitors every year. The serene ambiance of the temple complex, surrounding the sacred pool, offers a tranquil retreat for devotees and tourists alike.

Gwalior Fort

Madhya Pradesh
Gwalior Fort, situated in the city of Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, is an imposing hill fort known for its rich historical and architectural significance. Dating back to the 8th century, the fort has witnessed the rule of several dynasties, including the Tomars, Mughals, Marathas, and British. It features impressive palaces, temples, and water tanks, offering a glimpse into the region's vibrant past and cultural heritage.
Twang
Arunachal Pradesh
Tawang, nestled amidst the lofty Himalayas in Arunachal Pradesh, is renowned for its scenic beauty, Buddhist monasteries, and vibrant culture. The Tawang Monastery, one of the largest monasteries in India, stands as a spiritual beacon atop a hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. The pristine lakes, lush valleys, and snow-capped peaks make Tawang a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
Alleppey
Kerala
Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is a picturesque town in the southern Indian state of Kerala, famous for its serene backwaters, lush paddy fields, and tranquil beaches. A houseboat cruise along the backwaters of Alleppey is a quintessential Kerala experience, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the region's natural beauty and traditional way of life. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held on the Punnamada Lake, is a major highlight, attracting spectators from far and wide.

Camel Safari

Rajasthan
Camel Safari in Rajasthan offers an unforgettable adventure through the majestic Thar Desert, known for its golden sands, rugged terrain, and vibrant culture. Exploring the desert landscape atop a camel provides a unique perspective, allowing travelers to witness breathtaking sunsets, visit remote villages, and experience the hospitality of the local communities. From Jaisalmer to Pushkar, Rajasthan's camel safaris offer an authentic glimpse into the region's rich heritage and nomadic lifestyle.

Sanchi Stupa

Madhya Pradesh
The Sanchi Stupa, located in the town of Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh, is an ancient Buddhist monument dating back to the 3rd century BCE. Built by Emperor Ashoka, the stupa is renowned for its exquisite carvings, intricate gateways (toranas), and the Great Stupa, which enshrines relics of Lord Buddha. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sanchi Stupa stands as a testament to India's rich Buddhist heritage and architectural prowess.
Umngot river
Meghalaya
The Umngot River, flowing through the scenic town of Dawki in Meghalaya, is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, which offer mesmerizing reflections of the surrounding landscape. Boating on the Umngot River is a popular activity, allowing visitors to witness the river's astounding clarity and vibrant aquatic life. With its emerald-green waters and picturesque surroundings, the Umngot River is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.
Vivekananda Rock Memorial
Tamil Nadu
Vivekananda Rock Memorial, situated on a small island off the coast of Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu, is a sacred monument dedicated to Swami Vivekananda, the renowned Hindu philosopher and spiritual leader. The memorial stands atop a rock formation in the midst of the Arabian Sea, offering panoramic views of the confluence of the Bay of Bengal, the Arabian Sea, and the Indian Ocean. A symbol of unity and spirituality, Vivekananda Rock Memorial attracts pilgrims and tourists seeking solace and inspiration.

Mehrangarh Fort

Rajasthan
Mehrangarh Fort, perched atop a rocky hill in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, is one of the largest and most magnificent forts in India. Built in the 15th century by Rao Jodha, the fort showcases impressive palaces, intricate carvings, and expansive courtyards, reflecting the grandeur of Rajput architecture and heritage. From its towering ramparts, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the "Blue City" of Jodhpur and the surrounding Thar Desert.

Hampi

Karnataka
Hampi, located in the northern part of Karnataka, is an ancient city renowned for its splendid ruins, historic temples, and dramatic landscape. Once the flourishing capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting history enthusiasts, architecture aficionados, and spiritual seekers from around the world. The surreal boulder-strewn terrain, interspersed with majestic temples and monuments, transports visitors to a bygone era of opulence and grandeur.
Mon
Myanmar
Mon, situated in southeastern Myanmar, is a historic town known for its rich cultural heritage and ancient pagodas. The town is named after the Mon people, an ethnic group with a significant presence in the region. Mon is home to several well-preserved Buddhist temples and stupas, including the iconic Kyaiktiyo Pagoda (Golden Rock), a revered pilgrimage site perched atop a granite boulder precariously balanced on the edge of a cliff. Visitors to Mon can explore its traditional markets, observe local craftsmanship, and immerse themselves in the timeless charm of this historic town.
Colva Beach
Goa
Colva Beach, located in South Goa, is one of the region's most popular and picturesque beaches. With its expansive stretch of golden sands, swaying palm trees, and azure waters, Colva Beach offers the perfect setting for relaxation and leisure. Visitors can indulge in sunbathing, beach volleyball, and water sports or take leisurely strolls along the shoreline. The beach is also dotted with shacks and stalls offering delicious Goan cuisine, refreshing beverages, and vibrant handicrafts, making it a vibrant hub for tourists and locals alike.
